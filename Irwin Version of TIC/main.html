Provenance used in Arts

The provenance in art is the documentation that confirms the authenticity of the art. It shows the chronological history of the art such as Certificate, Exhibition sticker, verbal statement from an aritst.


For example, When you purchase an art piece, the inventory number will be stored with owner's information on database. Thus having a database with the information, when the art goes missing or a situation where need to verify real or fake art piece, the art gallery or researchers will be able to track the art.

Provenance in Law enforcement

In forensics, provenance becomes the source of the evidences. It is a documentation of the steps taken to process the evidences during the investigation. This includes how the evidences is transported copied, and stored.


For example, when a crime happens, investigators collects the evidences which is the data provenance. It divides into physical evidences and digital evidences. Physical evidences are gathered in evidence box physically. On the other hand, digital evidences are collected as digital data such as user history of victim/suspects, encoded information from the suspects. It is very had to document and record since it can be remotely accessed, copied and transferred. Later on, these provenances are used in the court to support the prosecution.

Provenance in Food Safety

In the food safety, the provenance takes an important role, traceability. The provenance stores the information about the supply chain. The food traceability needs provenance for manufacturers and consumers. For manufacturers, the provenance helps to lower the cost distribution. Also it helps to reduce the recall expenses as well as expand the product sales. For consumers, it helps to increase the safety of the food.


For example, we can trace back the cause of the defective parts through provenance. When Chipotle in Everett had E-coli outbreak, FDA was able to find the cause through food traceability using the provenance.
